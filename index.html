Making a Convincing Argument

Backfire Effect

Both the video and debunking handbook talk about the backfire effect that can occur while trying to convince someone that information they previously thought was true, actually was a myth. In the backfire effect, the target that believes the misconception can very easily forget what the misconception is if it is not presented carefully. In other instances, the target could also out right deny that they are wrong in the first place. Additionally, Cairo emphasizes that the way information is presented is just as important as the information itself. Although he is primarily talking about visualizations, this is consistent with the message the other two sources communicate. It is important that information, and especially misconceptions, are relayed in a way that does not end up with a backfire effect. One way to avoid the backfire effect is to not overload the target audience with too much information but to make sure to give a small amount of new information to replace the gap left by the misconception.
